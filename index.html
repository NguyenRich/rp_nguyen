The Mitigation of Acidification with the Presence of Polysiphonia

R. Nguyen, C. Queenan


Background

Polysiphonia - a red algae
Ocean Acidification - the decrease in pH in the ocean

  • Ocean acidification results from rising carbon emissions
  • Algae use photosynthesis to produce energy
  • Algae captures carbon dioxide and raises pH

Rationale

Ocean acidification is a worsening issue that needs to be counteracted.

The study...

  • provides an understanding of possible methods for mitigating acidification
  • provides a basis for how Polysiphonia may affect the waters in an acidified environment

Conclusion

Data indicated higher changes in pH when in a low pH environment.

The study...

  • indicates possibility of Polysiphonia being a way to counteract acidification
  • provided a basis for how Polysiphonia may be used to higher pH, and may lead to further research
